Our Story

Born on the farm, shaped by the landscape

Wild Canvas is a family-run luxury glamping experience set on our 700-hectare, third-generation farm in Waitomo. Designed to feel both elevated and deeply connected to nature, each private hilltop tent offers complete seclusion, panoramic views, and a chance to slow down and reconnect.

The Idea

The comforts of indoors, with the feeling of outdoors

Wild Canvas was born from a love of the landscape and a belief that comfort and nature can exist beautifully together.

There is something about sleeping beneath canvas that feels different. You remain connected to the world outside, hearing the wind move through the hills, waking to birdsong and the quiet rhythm of farm life nearby.

When we designed Wild Canvas, we didn't want to create a hotel placed in nature. We wanted to create an experience that felt immersed in it.

Each tent sits on its own hilltop, carefully positioned for privacy, views and a sense of escape. Here, mornings arrive slowly, sunsets stretch across the valleys, and evenings are spent beside the fire or beneath a sky full of stars.

“We believe there’s something special about sleeping beneath canvas. A feeling that connects you to the land in a way no building can.”

Meet Your Hosts

Emily & Benjamin

Wild Canvas is our family-run glamping experience, set on Benjamin's third-generation Waitomo farm.

Together we bring a mix of farming, engineering, design and hospitality, but more than anything, a genuine love for this landscape and sharing it with others.

From the hilltop locations and off-grid systems to the thoughtful details waiting inside each tent, Wild Canvas has been shaped by many years of dreaming, designing and building.

We feel incredibly lucky to call this place home and hope your time here feels just as special.

How We Got Here

The Wild Canvas journey

2016

A new chapter on the farm

Ben returns to help run his family's third-generation Waitomo farm full time, beginning to explore how the landscape could be shared.

2017

Waitomo Hilltop Glamping begins

Originally launched as Waitomo Hilltop Glamping, our first hilltop safari tent opened with a simple vision: to create somewhere beautifully designed, private and deeply connected to nature. Inspired by Emily's South African roots and a shared love of sleeping beneath canvas, it quickly became one of New Zealand's most loved glamping stays.

2022

A new name, the same vision

Waitomo Hilltop Glamping becomes Wild Canvas, reflecting a growing vision for thoughtful design, private hilltop escapes and experiences grounded in nature.

2023

The Pōhutukawa Tent opens

After two years of planning, engineering and construction, the Pōhutukawa Tent opens. Featuring four queen bedrooms, a cedar hot tub, indoor stone fireplace and a sophisticated off-grid energy system, it marks a bold new chapter for Wild Canvas.

2023

Airbnb Best Design Stay Award

Wild Canvas receives Airbnb's Best Design Stay Award, recognising the design, atmosphere and guest experience that had become synonymous with the Kōwhai Tent.

2024

5-Star Qualmark Gold

Wild Canvas achieves Qualmark 5-Star Glamping status alongside the prestigious Gold Sustainable Tourism Business Award, recognising both guest experience and a strong commitment to sustainability.

2025

A new Kōwhai Tent

The Kōwhai Tent is completely reimagined and relaunched with an indoor fireplace, polished concrete floors, twin claw-foot baths and a new native landscape design, creating a fresh chapter for the couples' retreat guests had already fallen in love with.

The Farm

Life on the land

Wild Canvas sits on our third-generation family farm in the heart of Waitomo, where rolling hills, grazing stock and pockets of native landscape shape daily life.

This is a working farm, home to cattle, sheep and the quiet rhythm of rural New Zealand. Beyond the tents, winter tree planting, native restoration and a deep connection to the land continue to shape the landscape for future generations.

From your private hilltop, you may spot sheep grazing below, hear birdsong drift through the valleys, and watch the landscape shift with changing light and weather. Farm life exists around you, while your stay remains entirely private and immersed in nature.
